Becky Pinnington

I'm a freelance data journalist specialising in investigations and creative pop culture projects. Recent work for The Observer includes investigating Reform UK's finances, digging into data quality issues at the Office for National Statistics, and scraping web data to determine the paper's Word of the Year.

Previously I spent five years with the Google News Initiative, where I worked on data-driven stories with journalists from The Economist, The Atlantic, and The Washington Post. My work with Nadieh Bremer on "Why do cats and dogs?" was nominated for an Information Is Beautiful award.

I have also worked as an embedded data journalist with Carwow, where my transport policy FOI investigations secured national media coverage.
